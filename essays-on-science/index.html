I think it is necessary to educate new generations to the value of defeat. To its management. To the humanity that flows from it. To build an identity capable of sensing a shared destiny—where one can fail and begin again without value and dignity being compromised. To not become a social climber, not to step over others' bodies to get there first. In this world of vulgar and dishonest winners, of false oppressors and opportunists, of people who matter, who hold power, who seize the present—let alone the future—of all those neurotic about success, about appearing, about becoming. To this anthropology of the victor, I vastly prefer whoever loses. It is an exercise I am good at. And it reconciles me with my sacred littleness.

— cit. Pier Paolo Pasolini
